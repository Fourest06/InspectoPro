Quality Assurance Check Sheets

# Question Yes No Remarks
QA Check Sheet Design and Approval
1Are QA check sheets available and specific to the work items or trades being audited?
2Were QA check sheets reviewed and approved by the QA/QC Manager and Project Engineer?
3Do the check sheets clearly indicate inspection hold points and acceptance criteria?
4Are the check sheets in accordance with the Quality Assurance Plan and ITP?
5Are revisions to check sheets controlled and properly documented?
6Are check sheets aligned with the latest construction drawings and specifications?
7Are there separate QA check sheets for critical work items (e.g., structural works, waterproofing)?
8Is there a system in place to request or approve new check sheets as new work activities arise?
Use and Implementation of Check Sheets on Site
1Are QA check sheets being used consistently for daily site activities?
2Are they being filled out accurately, legibly, and completely by authorized personnel?
3Are inspection items signed by responsible inspectors or engineers at each stage?
4Are actual values (e.g., dimensions, test results) recorded where applicable?
5Are discrepancies or non-conforming results identified and highlighted in the sheets?
6Are hold points clearly observed before proceeding to the next activity?
7Are client or consultant representatives signing or acknowledging applicable inspections?
8Are site supervisors aware of the specific QA check sheets they are expected to use?
9Are filled-out check sheets kept with the latest site documents and accessible for audit?
10Are daily work activities supported by completed QA check sheets?
Monitoring and Documentation Control
1Are all QA check sheets filed and organized according to discipline or trade?
2Is there a log or tracking system for issued and completed QA check sheets?
3Are incomplete or missing check sheets flagged and followed up by the QA/QC team?
4Are original copies stored in a secure and accessible location (e.g., QA Office)?
5Are check sheets cross-referenced with work inspection requests (WIRs) or NCRs?
6Are QA check sheets subject to periodic internal audits for completeness and accuracy?
7Are scanned or digital copies used where electronic filing is required?
8Are document control procedures being followed for the archiving of check sheets?
9Are outdated versions removed from use and labeled as obsolete?
10Are QA check sheets available during site walkthroughs and third-party audits?
Integration with Inspection and Testing Plans (ITP)
1Are QA check sheets developed based on the ITP for the project?
2Are the required inspection stages in the ITP reflected in the check sheets?
3Are inspection and test requirements consistent between ITPs and check sheets?
4Are inspectors using ITPs and check sheets together when performing inspections?
5Are results from check sheets used to validate compliance with the ITP?
6Are changes in ITPs reflected promptly in the related check sheets?
7Are ITP reference numbers or codes indicated in the QA check sheets?
8Are failed ITP inspections supported by corresponding QA check sheet entries?
Continuous Improvement and Lessons Learned
1Are there regular reviews of QA check sheet data to identify trends or recurring issues?
2Are lessons learned from QA check sheet reviews discussed in QA/QC meetings?
3Are corrective actions based on check sheet findings documented and followed up?
4Is there feedback from engineers, foremen, or inspectors on improving check sheet formats?
5Are check sheets revised based on post-activity evaluations or rework analysis?
6Are errors in filled check sheets communicated for retraining or process adjustment?
7Are lessons learned from check sheet audits shared across projects or teams?
8Is QA leadership reviewing the adequacy of existing check sheets during project milestones?
