Project Inspection As-Built Packages

# Question Yes No Remarks
As-Built Documentation Management
1Are up-to-date as-built drawings available and tagged according to revisions?
2Are as-built plans prepared by the contractor and verified by the site engineer or inspector?
3Is there a formal process for reviewing, signing, and approving as-built drawings?
4Are as-built drawings submitted in both hard copy and editable electronic format (e.g., AutoCAD)?
5Are layout changes, deviations, or field modifications properly reflected in the as-builts?
6Are redline drawings maintained daily and transitioned accurately into final as-builts?
7Are structural, architectural, MEP, and utility drawings included in the as-built set?
8Are as-builts stored securely and accessible to the client and project stakeholders?
Inspection Records and Final Acceptance
1Are inspection request forms (IRFs) completed for all major construction activities?
2Are all IRFs approved and signed by the consultant/client representative before inclusion in the turnover package?
3Are punch list items generated during inspection properly tracked and closed out?
4Are inspection records clearly linked to work activity logs and drawings?
5Are witness or hold-point inspections documented with signatures and timestamps?
6Are material test results (e.g., concrete compressive strength, rebar test) included in the inspection package?
7Are all test reports authenticated by an accredited third-party laboratory where applicable?
8Are inspection reports organized by discipline (civil, electrical, plumbing, etc.)?
9Are photographic records of inspections and installations included in the turnover documentation?
10Are final inspection forms signed off by the Engineer-of-Record or QA/QC head?
Project Close-out and Turnover Package Compilation
1Is there a documented checklist for all documents required in the project close-out package?
2Are final completion reports prepared and submitted in line with the contract or agency standards (e.g., DPWH guidelines)?
3Are all operating manuals, warranties, and maintenance instructions included in the as-built package?
4Are final bill of quantities (BOQ) reconciled with as-built drawings and actual work?
5Are subcontractor documentation packages integrated into the main turnover file?
6Are “no objection” certificates or client approval forms attached to the as-built package?
7Are permits and clearances (e.g., occupancy, fire, DENR ECC, DOLE compliance) attached as part of the final documentation?
8Are scanned/digital copies submitted with proper indexing and navigation (e.g., bookmarked PDFs)?
9Are certificates of completion and final acceptance prepared and approved?
10Is the entire turnover package archived per the project documentation retention policy?
Quality Control & Documentation Accuracy
1Are as-built drawings cross-checked against actual site conditions by the QA/QC team?
2Are inconsistencies between redline drawings and final as-builts addressed and corrected?
3Are dimensional checks or surveyed coordinates included in the final drawings?
4Are deviations from the approved IFC (Issued for Construction) drawings explained and approved?
5Are technical data sheets and materials used reconciled with inspection reports?
6Are quality assurance records (e.g., ITPs, NCRs) included in the documentation?
7Are any identified deficiencies in the documentation flagged for corrective action before turnover?
8Are installation records and commissioning reports (if applicable) part of the documentation package?
9Are as-built submissions reviewed by both engineering and quality departments prior to client submittal?
10Are document version controls and transmittals properly tracked?
Client Review, Final Audit, and Archiving
1Was a formal document review conducted by the client or consultant for the final as-built package?
2Are all client comments and punch list observations resolved prior to final handover?
3Was a close-out meeting or presentation conducted to walk through the as-built package?
4Are turnover documents organized in a way that complies with the project document control system?
5Is there a sign-off sheet or approval form for the complete as-built package from all parties?
6Are final project files archived in accordance with company and client policy (physical/digital)?
7Are as-built files stored in a central repository accessible for future maintenance or warranty work?
8Was feedback on the as-built and inspection process gathered from the client for continuous improvement?
9Are data files backed up and protected from loss or damage (e.g., via cloud storage or external drives)?
10Are lessons learned from the as-built and turnover documentation process compiled for future reference?
