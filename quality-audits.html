Quality Audits Template

# Question Yes No Remarks
Quality Audit Planning and Preparation
1Is there an annual or project-specific internal quality audit plan in place?
2Are audit schedules communicated in advance to all relevant project teams?
3Are the scope, criteria, and objectives of the quality audit clearly defined?
4Is the audit plan aligned with ISO 9001, project-specific QCP, or DPWH guidelines?
5Are qualified and independent auditors assigned for each audit activity?
6Is an opening meeting conducted to discuss audit purpose and process?
7Are audit checklists or tools prepared prior to the actual audit?
8Are auditees informed of their roles and expected documents for the audit?
Execution of Quality Audits (Internal and External)
1Are quality audits conducted as per the planned schedule or with valid justification for delays?
2Are records of internal audits (forms, observations, checklists) maintained and signed?
3Are external audits (e.g., by consultants, ISO certifiers, or owners) facilitated properly?
4Are all major work disciplines (civil, electrical, architectural, etc.) included in the audits?
5Are field observations and interviews conducted during audits?
6Are audit findings clearly classified (e.g., observation, minor non-conformance, major non-conformance)?
7Are non-conformities supported with evidence (e.g., photos, logs, or test reports)?
8Are auditors impartial and not auditing their own work or department?
9Are sub-contractors or suppliers also included in quality audits?
10Are audit checklists updated based on lessons learned from previous audits?
Reporting and Documentation of Audit Results
1Are audit reports prepared within a reasonable time after the audit?
2Do audit reports include detailed findings, recommendations, and responsible parties?
3Are audit results reviewed with site management or project leadership?
4Are findings presented in a clear, actionable format (e.g., Corrective Action Request)?
5Are NCRs or audit observations logged and tracked systematically?
6Are audit documents (plans, reports, findings) filed and backed up electronically or physically?
7Are results from audits shared across other teams or projects for benchmarking?
8Are recurring findings from audits monitored across audit cycles?
9Are audit records accessible to the client or consultant if required?
10Is there a documented process for reviewing and approving audit reports?
Corrective and Preventive Actions
1Are corrective action plans prepared for each audit non-conformance?
2Are root cause analyses conducted for major or repeated findings?
3Are timelines and responsible persons clearly defined in the corrective action plan?
4Are corrective actions validated and verified for effectiveness?
5Are follow-up audits or spot checks conducted to confirm implementation?
6Are preventive actions (not just corrective) considered based on audit data?
7Are unresolved issues escalated to senior management for intervention?
8Are lessons learned used to improve processes or revise QCP/ITPs?
9Are non-conformance close-out reports signed by the QA/QC Manager?
10Are repeated audit failures addressed through retraining or procedural changes?
Audit Review, Analysis, and Continuous Improvement
1Are audit findings analyzed for trends across departments or time periods?
2Are quality audit summaries presented in management review meetings?
3Is the internal audit process reviewed and improved based on experience?
4Are audit results used to improve subcontractor or supplier quality requirements?
5Are there performance indicators (e.g., % of closed NCRs) monitored regularly?
6Is auditor performance reviewed for impartiality and effectiveness?
7Are opportunities for improvement (OFIs) taken seriously and implemented?
8Are positive findings recognized and shared to encourage best practices?
9Are quality audits helping to reduce cost of poor quality (COPQ) over time?
10Is the audit program helping the organization meet its overall quality objectives?
